PAPER BLADES

Level Designer

OVERVIEW

PAPER BLADES is a tactical action roguelite that blends RTS elements with strategic gameplay, where players command origami warriors in a feudal Japanese setting. As the Level Designer for this project, my role focused on creating engaging tactical environments that complement the game’s unique origami aesthetic and Japanese feudal architecture.

The game combines traditional Japanese folklore with innovative paper-craft visuals, presenting a distinctive art style in which characters and environments appear to be made from folded paper. These elements clash with dynamic 3D procedurally generated enemies, enhancing the contrast and visual intrigue.

My responsibilities included designing an island-like terrain suitable for 2D character movement, as well as creating a village inspired by feudal Japanese architecture—with a papery feel to reinforce the immersive paper world and the looming invasion of the INK. To suit the roguelite nature of the game, I also focused on building semi-randomized level layouts that enhance replayability and keep gameplay fresh.

Working on PAPER BLADES was a valuable experience, as it was one of my first major projects. It taught me the importance of teamwork, adapting to a fast-paced development environment, and the reality of crunch time sometimes working up to 15 hours a da and night. The project was completed in just three months and marked the first official title developed by our team at PlaceHolders Studio.

GOALS

  • Design tactical arenas that encourage strategic unit positioning and movement
  • Create environments that complement the paper=origami aesthetic while maintaining gameplay clarity
  • Develop level layouts that support both RTS-style unit management and action-oriented combat
  • Make a terrain that will not collide with a 2D player
  • Ensure levels work effectively with the roguelite progression and randomization systems

RESTRICTIONS

  • Maintain visual consistency with the paper-craft art style throughout all level elements
  • Limited development resources for creating custom environmental assets
  • Camera constraints required careful consideration of level verticality and depth
  • Very unique blend of genres making it hard to search for inspiration
  • Short time frame of 3 months
